Pension Architects

The Total Solution Approach


Stage 1: Identify Plan Design Characteristics

  • Exploratory Meeting - Initial meeting with plan sponsor to discuss their needs and our design capabilities.
  • PEA - Conduct an onsite Plan Efficiency Assessment with plan sponsor to document and explore current plan specifics, as well as identify strengths and weaknesses of the current model.
  • Visualization - Review and discuss initial design requests and parameters with plan sponsor and our team.

Stage 2: Draft a Set of Plans

  • First Look - Present plan sponsor with the first draft of our in-depth analysis of their Plan for review and comment.
  • Site Tour - Provide references of other comparable projects for plan sponsor to contact prior to adopting their new design along with a systems demonstration.
  • Re-Design - Modify and re-draft plans to reflect comments and additional requests of the plan sponsor in order to develop a model for final consideration.

Stage 3: Build and Execute Plan Design

  • Break Ground - Upon approval of plan design the transition process starts approximately 60 days prior to desired conversion date. Plan documents and design are operationalized, systems are brought online, conversion timeline and educational visits are laid out.
  • Lay Foundation - — Starts approximately 30 days prior to conversion date. Familiarize participants with the enhanced features and “Retirement Goal Planning” services of their new plan through orientations.
  • Set the Cornerstone - — Occurs a few weeks prior to conversion date. Work privately with each participant and spouse/significant other to initiate “Retirement Goal Planning” process.
  • Progress Review - Onsite meeting shortly after conversion to further explore systems capabilities and present “Post-Conversion Review” analysis.

Stage 4: Evaluate and Maintain the Project

  • Annual Plan Review - Occurs at least annually (available more frequently by request) to document and review the plan in accordance with the Investment Policy Statement.
  • Quarterly Site Visits - Available each quarter to meet with newly eligible and, existing participants to answer questions about the plan, help them enroll, update or initiate financial planning, or discuss distribution options.
  • Administrative Review - Onsite administrative assistance provided to discuss or review systems.
